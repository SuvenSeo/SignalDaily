# SignalDaily Quality Standard

SignalDaily should feel like a compact intelligence desk, not a generic news dump.

## Scoring rubric

Score each full daily brief out of 100.

| Area | Points | Standard |
|---|---:|---|
| Signal selection | 20 | The brief chooses the highest-impact developments, not random headlines. |
| Source quality | 20 | Primary sources are prioritized and factual claims are linked. |
| Analysis quality | 15 | Facts, interpretation, and uncertainty are clearly separated. |
| Sri Lanka relevance | 15 | Local economy/CSE context is useful, careful, and not financial advice. |
| Builder usefulness | 15 | Opportunity Radar gives practical project, product, and learning ideas. |
| Structure and readability | 10 | Sections are consistent, concise, and easy to scan. |
| Archive hygiene | 5 | Correct filename, date, sources, and commit message. |

## Definition of high signal

A signal is worth including when at least one of these is true:

- It changes what builders should learn, build, or monitor.
- It affects AI/model/coding-tool workflows.
- It affects Sri Lanka macro, CSE context, policy, FX, tourism, remittances, exports, banking, or listed-company risk.
- It changes global market risk, capital flows, AI infrastructure, commodities, rates, or crypto context.
- It creates a practical project, automation, research, or startup opportunity.

## Daily brief minimum bar

Each daily brief must include:

- 5 executive-summary signals.
- At least 5 credible source links.
- A dedicated Sri Lanka/CSE/economy section.
- A no-financial-advice disclaimer in market sections.
- At least 7 Opportunity Radar ideas across coding, SaaS, Sri Lanka, and market research.
- A deep dive with clear next-watch items.
- A Telegram-ready alert.

## Failure conditions

Reject or revise the brief if it:

- Gives buy/sell/hold instructions for securities, crypto, currencies, or commodities.
- Uses weak claims like "experts say" without naming sources.
- Hides uncertainty.
- Uses old data while implying it is current.
- Mixes facts and speculation in the same bullet.
- Includes unsourced price, index, FX, policy, or company-disclosure claims.
- Has no actionable builder angle.

## Editorial style

- Direct, concise, and evidence-first.
- Use exact dates.
- Prefer bullets over long paragraphs.
- Explain acronyms on first use when they are not obvious.
- Avoid hype words unless quoted from a source.
- Mark interpretation explicitly.
