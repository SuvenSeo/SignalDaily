# Source Quality Rules

SignalDaily should use a source hierarchy. Prefer higher-trust sources when available.

## Tier 1: primary / official sources

Use these first for factual claims:

- Official company blogs and announcements
- Official documentation
- Central Bank of Sri Lanka publications
- Colombo Stock Exchange announcements
- SEC Sri Lanka notices
- Listed-company disclosures
- GitHub official blog and repositories
- Government/statistical agencies
- Original research papers

## Tier 2: reputable reporting

Use these for context and broader coverage:

- Reuters
- Bloomberg
- CNBC
- Financial Times
- The Verge
- TechCrunch
- The Information
- MIT Technology Review
- IEEE Spectrum
- reputable local Sri Lankan business/news outlets

## Tier 3: discovery and sentiment

Use these carefully. They are useful for discovery, not final authority:

- Hacker News
- Reddit
- X/Twitter posts
- Product Hunt
- Indie Hackers
- personal blogs
- newsletters

## Citation rules

- Every factual claim should be traceable to a source.
- Prefer direct source links over secondhand summaries.
- Do not overstate a source's conclusion.
- If sources disagree, state the disagreement.
- If information is not confirmed, mark it as unconfirmed.
- Do not cite low-quality sources for high-stakes claims.

## Market information rules

For Sri Lanka/CSE/markets:

- Prefer CSE, CBSL, SEC Sri Lanka, official company disclosures, and audited reports.
- Do not rely on social media rumors.
- Do not frame watchlist items as recommendations.
- Use educational language: "monitor," "research," "risk," "catalyst," "possible interpretation."

## Confidence levels

Use these labels:

- **High:** official source or multiple strong sources agree
- **Medium:** reputable reporting but not yet confirmed by primary source
- **Low:** early signal, limited confirmation, or sentiment-based discovery
